
# 🧪 Task 1: Phishing Email Simulation (Mastercard Cybersecurity Program)

---

## 📌 Objective

Examine an obvious fake email and make it more believable as part of a phishing awareness simulation.

---

## 📧 Phishing Email (Original Analysis)

**From:** ITSupport@mastercard.com  
**To:** employee@mastercard.com  
**Subject:** Immediate Action Required: Password Reset  

Dear [Employee Name],

We have detected unusual activity on your email account, which suggests it may have been compromised. To ensure the security of your account and protect sensitive company information, we require you to reset your password immediately.

Please follow the link below to reset your password within the next hour. Failure to do so will result in your account being temporarily locked for security reasons.

Reset Your Password

If you have any questions or need further assistance, please contact our IT support team.

Best regards,  
Mastercard IT Support  

---

## ⚠️ Phishing Indicators Identified

- Urgent language to pressure immediate action  
- Threat of account lockout within a short timeframe  
- Generic greeting instead of personalized name  
- Suspicious password reset link  
- Psychological manipulation (fear + urgency)  

---

## 🛠️ Improved (More Believable Simulation Version)

**From:** IT Security Team <security@mastercard-support.com>  
**Subject:** Security Alert: Account Verification Required  

Dear Employee,

As part of Mastercard’s ongoing security monitoring, we have detected an unusual login attempt on your account.

For your protection, please verify your account activity using the secure portal below:

https://secure-mastercard-login.com/verify

This verification is required within 24 hours to ensure continued access to your account.

If you did not attempt any recent login, please contact the IT Security team immediately.

Regards,  
Mastercard Security Operations Center  

---

## 📊 Outcome

This task demonstrates how attackers use urgency and impersonation to trick users. The improved version highlights how realistic phishing emails can be crafted for awareness training purposes.
